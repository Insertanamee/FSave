<b>aA + bB ⇌ cC + dD</b>

#### Homogeneous systems and K<sub>c</sub>

* A <b>homogeneous </b>system is where all of the reactants and products are in the <b>same physical state</b>, e.g.

CH<sub>3</sub>COOH (l) + C<sub>2</sub>H<sub>5</sub>OH (l) ⇌ CH<sub>3</sub>COOC<sub>2</sub>H<sub>5</sub> (l) + H<sub>2</sub>O (l)

#### Heterogeneous systems and K<sub>c</sub>

* A <b>heterogeneous </b>system is where not all of the reactants and products are in the same physical state, e.g.

CaCO<sub>3</sub> (s) ⇌ CaO (s) + CO<sub>2</sub> (g)

* <b>Solids</b> are ignored in equilibrium expressions

  + This leads to a *K*<sub>c</sub> expression of *K*<sub>c</sub> = [CO<sub>2</sub>]

#### Examiner Tips and Tricks

For *K*<sub>c</sub> expressions, it is important that you use square brackets as sometimes examiners are instructed to be strict about the appearance of brackets in expressions

Square brackets implies concentration

#### Examiner Tips and Tricks

The value of *K*<sub>c</sub> indicates the position of equilibrium:

*K*<sub>c</sub> = 1 - the position of equilibrium is halfway between the reactants and products

*K*<sub>c</sub> < 1 - the position of equilibrium lies towards the left-hand side, i.e. the reactants

*K*<sub>c</sub> > 1 - the position of equilibrium lies towards the right-hand side, i.e. the products
